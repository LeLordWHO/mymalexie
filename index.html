  2. Tomorrow and Tomorrow and Tomorrow — Gabrielle Zevin
    Jeudi 2 Novembre 2023 à 20h00
    Summary : Opinion was very divided on this book. While all agree that the prose was bland and uninspired, certain readers felt there were valuable perspectives and themes developed within. In particular, it's nice to see a decent portrayal of video games in a book. Similarly to shows like Friends are Grey's Anatomy, the book can be easily consumed, and perhaps forgotten. Nevertheless, the author has sparked a newfound interest in video games among our members, and for that, they deserve our commendation. Le repas.
  3. Alice's Adventures in Wonderland and Through the Looking Glass — Lewis Carroll
    Mercredi 20 Septembre 2023 à 10h00
    Summary : The book elicited a range of reactions from our club members. While certain members were absolutely enamored, finding it a delightful embodiment of the joyous process wherein an adult shares his enchanting vision of life with a child, others were less enthusiastic (a notable critique for some was the perceived lack of a strong narrative thread). The tea-party scene was a standout for many, with its fascinating alternative logical rules, as well as the encounter with the Caterpillar, delving into themes of identity and transformation, echoing another popular moment where a bird mistakes Alice for a serpent. The Brunch.
  4. L'Analphabète & Le grand cahier — Agota Kristof
    Lundi 17 août 2023 à 10h30
    Summary : Chacun s'accorde à dire que Le Grand Cahier a su captiver avec son style épuré, son ambiance surréaliste, ses personnages hors du commun et ses rebondissements inattendus, le tout apporté par une narration en première personne plurielle. Concernant 'l'Analphabète', un membre a été particulièrement marqué par la divergence entre ses valeurs et celles de l'autrice, une perception qui a d'ailleurs teinté sa lecture de Le Grand Cahier.   5. The Round House: A Novel — Louise Erdrich
    Mercredi 14 juin 2023 à 14h00
    Summary : Everyone enjoyed this book. Particular highlights were: the context of a first nation reservation (for many of us, a relatively unknown culture), the theme of justice (in its many forms and with its many limitations), the mixture of multiple spiritual systems and the beautiful descriptions of a young teenager wrestling with his emotions surrounding parental strife. The book captures a summer of extreme trauma that simultaneously contains many moments of simply being a teenager with your friends over the summer holidays. Afternoon Tea..
  6. Des éclairs — Jean Echenoz
    Lundi 9 mai 2023 à 10h30.
    Synthèse : Les avis sont partagés. Alors que certains apprécient la prose aérée, d’autres la trouve trop simple.
